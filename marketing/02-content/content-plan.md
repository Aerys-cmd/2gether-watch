# 2gether Watch Content Plan — Phase 1

Source: `marketing/01-research/keyword-research.md` — single source of truth, not re-derived here.

Scope: first 12 pages, chosen for signup-conversion odds over raw traffic.

Future writing agents: read this file only. Generate ONE article at a time, in Publishing Order. Update its Status row when done. Do not batch-generate.

## Why these 12

Three buckets, each tied directly to the research file's rankings:

- **Comparison / brand-defense pages (6)** — the research file's Highest Priority Keywords list is dominated by "[competitor] alternative" and "vs" searches (#1-6 on that list). These convert best because the searcher already wants to switch tools; they just need a reason to pick this one. The no-sign-up and free-tool pages sit in this bucket too because they're brand-defense in disguise — every major competitor gates something behind an account or a paywall, so "no sign up" and "free, no ads" are direct rebuttals to Watch2Gether, Rave, Scener, and Kast.
- **Developer / self-hosted pages (3)** — smaller audience, but the research file flags this cluster explicitly as high-value-per-visitor: self-hosters read READMEs, star repos, and link from their own blogs. None of the closed-source competitors can contest these keywords at all.
- **Pillar / use-case pages (3)** — the YouTube-sync and long-distance/video-chat clusters from the research file. These capture searchers who haven't picked a specific competitor to defect from yet, and they link back into the comparison and self-hosted pages once those are live.

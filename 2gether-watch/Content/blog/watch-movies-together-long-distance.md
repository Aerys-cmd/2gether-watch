Long-distance movie nights usually mean running two apps at once — a video call in one window and a "watch together" extension or site in another, hoping they don't drift out of sync with each other or with the video itself.

[2gether Watch](/) puts both in the same room, so there's one link to share instead of two apps to coordinate.

## What a long-distance movie night actually needs

- **Reliable sync** — nothing kills the mood faster than being three seconds ahead of the person you're watching with.
- **Seeing each other, not just hearing** — a webcam feed alongside the video makes it feel like a shared room, not a scheduled call.
- **Low setup friction** — on a regular weekly movie night, neither of you wants to re-explain how to join an account-gated tool every time.

## How it works as a routine

1. One person [creates a room](/) and sends the link.
2. Both of you toggle your cameras and microphones on from the footer controls.
3. Paste the YouTube link or direct video URL and load it.
4. Watch, talk, react in real time — the call runs peer-to-peer alongside the synced video, in the same tab.

Since there's no account on either end, there's nothing to keep re-logging into every week — the routine is just "open the link."

## Screen sharing for anything that isn't a clean video link

Not everything you want to watch together has a tidy shareable URL. The screen-share toggle covers that case — share your screen instead of loading a video URL, and the other person sees exactly what you see.

## Privacy, since it's a personal call

Because calls run over peer-to-peer WebRTC rather than through a server, your video and audio aren't relayed through or stored on 2gether Watch's infrastructure — they go directly between your two browsers. See the [privacy page](/Privacy) for the full breakdown of what the app does and doesn't handle.

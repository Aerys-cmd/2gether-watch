There isn't one "best" watch party app — there's a best one for what you're actually trying to do. Here's how the major options stack up as of 2026.

## The field

- **Watch2Gether** — general-purpose sync rooms, wide video-source support, free with ads, account required for saved rooms and history, paid tier for HD sync and larger rooms. See our [full Watch2Gether comparison](/blog/watch2gether-alternative).
- **Teleparty** (formerly Netflix Party) — Chrome extension that syncs playback inside supported streaming platforms with a chat sidebar. No calls, no screen share, no support for arbitrary video URLs. See our [full Teleparty comparison](/blog/teleparty-alternative).
- **Scener** — browser extension for a "virtual theater" experience on Netflix, Disney+, Prime Video, and Max, with webcam tiles. Requires an account and extension; limited free tier.
- **Rave** — mobile-first app with group video chat bubbles. Requires an account/phone number; media is routed through Rave's servers; freemium paywall for larger groups.
- **Kast** — desktop app aimed at gamers and Discord-adjacent communities, strong screen-share UX. Requires a download and account; closed source.
- **Metastream** — open source, Electron-based synced player. Free, no account, but no built-in calls or chat — most groups pair it with a separate Discord call.
- **2gether Watch** — browser-based, no install, no account, synced YouTube/direct video URLs, built-in calls, chat, and screen sharing, up to 10 viewers, open source and self-hostable.

## How to pick

- **Want the least friction, no account, and calls built in:** [2gether Watch](/).
- **Watching mostly on Netflix/Disney+/Hulu and just want sync + chat:** Teleparty.
- **Need the widest possible source support and don't mind an account:** Watch2Gether.
- **Want a "virtual theater" feel specifically on major streaming platforms:** Scener.
- **Already self-host everything and use Discord for voice:** Metastream.

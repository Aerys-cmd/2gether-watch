A lot of watch-party tools are free the way a mobile game is free — the core loop is free, and then room size, sync quality, or saved history gets fenced off behind a subscription once you're using it regularly.

[2gether Watch](/) doesn't have a premium tier to hit. There's one version of the app, and everyone gets the same features: synced video, chat, calls, and screen sharing, for free, with no ads shown anywhere in the room.

## Where the free tier usually stops on other apps

- **Watch2Gether** — free tier is ad-supported, with HD sync and larger rooms behind a paid plan.
- **Rave** — free tier caps group size, with a subscription to unlock more.
- **Scener** — limited free tier before premium features kick in.

## Why 2gether Watch doesn't have this problem

There's no billing system, no account tier to upgrade, and no usage limit to hit — the free/premium split doesn't exist because there's nothing to sell you after the free version. Rooms support up to 10 viewers by default (a technical ceiling on the full-mesh WebRTC connection each peer opens to every other peer, not a feature you'd pay to raise), and every feature — sync, chat, calls, screen share — is available in every room.

## The honest tradeoff

Free with no paid tier also means no dedicated support line, no SLA, and no roadmap driven by what a paying segment wants. For a casual watch-party tool, that trade is usually worth it. If you need guaranteed uptime for a commercial use case, that's a different product category entirely.

Watch2Gether has been around for a long time and it works — but it also asks for an account if you want saved rooms or history, runs ads, and puts HD sync and larger rooms behind a paid plan. If you just want to open a room and press play, that's a lot of friction for something that should take ten seconds.

[2gether Watch](/) is built around the opposite idea: no account, ever. You open the homepage, optionally type a display name, and click **Create New Room**. That's the whole signup flow.

## What's actually different

| | 2gether Watch | Watch2Gether |
|---|---|---|
| Account required | No, never | Not for basic use, but required for saved rooms/history |
| Ads | None | Yes, on the free tier |
| Price | Free, no premium tier | Freemium — HD sync and larger rooms are paywalled |
| Audio/video calls | Built in, peer-to-peer | Not built in |
| Screen sharing | Built in | Not built in |
| Media routing | Peer-to-peer WebRTC — never touches the server | Routed through Watch2Gether's servers |
| Source | Open source (MIT), self-hostable | Closed source |

## Why the "no account" part matters more than it sounds

It's not just about skipping a form. Because 2gether Watch never stores an account, there's nothing tied to your identity beyond the room link itself — and because audio, video, and screen-share media travel directly between browsers over WebRTC rather than through a server, the app never sees or stores your camera, microphone, or screen feed. Watch2Gether's model works fine for playback sync, but it doesn't offer that same peer-to-peer privacy story for calls, mostly because it doesn't offer calls at all.

## Everything you'd actually use it for

- **Sync** — paste a YouTube link or a direct video URL and everyone in the room watches in lockstep.
- **Chat** — a live text chat panel next to the video.
- **Calls** — optional webcam and microphone, so you can talk while you watch.
- **Screen share** — share your screen instead of a video URL, useful for anything that isn't a clean streaming link.
- **Up to 10 viewers** per room, full mesh, no server relay for media.

## Switching over

There's nothing to migrate — no account to close, no history to export. Go to the [homepage](/), create a room, and send the link to whoever you were about to invite to a Watch2Gether session instead.

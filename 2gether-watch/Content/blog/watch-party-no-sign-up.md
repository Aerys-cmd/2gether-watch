"No sign-up required" shows up in a lot of watch-party marketing, but it usually means "no sign-up to start" — the account wall shows up the moment you want to save a room, invite more than a couple of people, or come back to your history later.

## What "no account, ever" actually looks like

[2gether Watch](/) has no account system at all — not a delayed one, not an optional one. There's no login form anywhere in the app. A room is just a URL: create one, share the link, and anyone with it can join. Your display name is stored in your own browser's local storage, not on a server, purely so it's remembered next time you visit.

## Where competitors draw the line

- **Watch2Gether** — no account needed for a quick room, but saved rooms and history require one.
- **Rave** — requires an account and phone number before you can create a room at all.
- **Scener** — requires an account and browser extension.
- **Kast** — requires an account and a desktop app download.
- **Teleparty** — no account required, but also no calls, no screen share, and it only works on supported streaming platforms.

## Why this is worth caring about

Every account is one more thing tied to your identity, one more password, one more thing that can leak in a breach. For something as low-stakes as a movie night with friends, that's a lot of overhead for a feature you'll use for two hours and then forget about. It also means anyone can join a room without first being talked into creating an account of their own — which matters more than it sounds when you're trying to get five people into a room at the same time.

## What you skip by not needing an account

- No email required
- No password to create or remember
- No "verify your email" step before the room even loads
- Nothing to delete later if you stop using it

If you just want to open a room and start watching, [go to the homepage](/), type a display name (optional), and click **Create New Room**.
